A Reflection

Ageing, Resilience & Supporting the Body We Live In

Since turning 60, I've become even more aware of how much our bodies change as we age. Things that once seemed simple, maintaining a healthy weight, recovering from a busy week, sleeping well, feeling energised, can suddenly require a lot more intention.

I've also come to appreciate how connected everything really is. Hormones influence so much more than we often realise: energy, sleep, mood, focus, libido, bone health and even how resilient we feel when life gets busy.

When I talk with women in my community, many are experiencing similar challenges, brain fog, poor sleep, low energy, inflammation, bloating, hormonal changes. A body that suddenly feels different to the one they've known for decades.

"Ageing well isn't about trying to turn back the clock. It's about supporting the body we have today."

For me, that means paying closer attention to the foundations: quality nutrition, prioritising protein, supporting gut health, building strength, getting enough rest and filling the nutritional gaps that become more apparent as we age.

And perhaps the biggest mindset shift of all has been learning to be kinder to myself. To rest when I need to rest. To appreciate what my body can do rather than focusing on what has changed. Because healthy ageing isn't about perfection, it's about resilience, consistency and giving our bodies the support they deserve.

Nutrition Focus 🥤

Why Protein Has Become Non-Negotiable for Me After 50

If there's one thing I've become more intentional about in my 50s and now into my 60s, it's protein. I originally started paying more attention to it because of the amount of exercise and strength training I was doing. What I didn't realise at the time was just how important protein becomes as we age.

What many women don't realise is that we naturally lose muscle as we age. That's why getting enough protein and incorporating some form of strength training becomes so important for healthy ageing, mobility, metabolism and maintaining independence.

For me, protein isn't about dieting, it's about supporting strength, healthy ageing, recovery and giving my body the building blocks it needs to thrive.

Practical Wellness Tip 💪

Why Strength Training Matters After 50

If there's one form of exercise I believe becomes increasingly important as we age, it's strength training, not because I want to look like a bodybuilder, but because I want to stay strong, capable and independent for as long as possible.

What I've noticed most isn't how I look, it's how I function. I feel stronger carrying groceries, climbing stairs, travelling, keeping up with everyday life.

"One of the biggest misconceptions I hear from women is that if they eat more protein and lift weights, they'll end up looking bulky. The reality? Most women who strength train simply become stronger, more capable and more confident."

Start where you are. You don't need a perfect program. You simply need to take the first step.

What I'm Loving Right Now 🍽️

Korean Beef Bowl

On high rotation in our house lately, protein-rich, family-friendly, tastes like takeaway.

This is one of those meals that feels a little indulgent, yet is surprisingly simple to make. The whole family loves it, it's great for leftovers and it's packed with protein.

Ingredients

Beef Bowl

  • 1 cup rice, uncooked
  • 500g (1 lb) ground beef
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup sriracha
  • 3/4 cup beef broth
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 heaping tsp grated ginger
  • 1 heaping tsp grated garlic
  • 1 medium yellow onion, sliced

Fried Egg

  • 1 egg per serving
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 2 tbsp sliced green onion (green and white parts)
  • 1 tsp chilli crisp

To Garnish

  • Extra sliced green onion
  • Sesame seeds

Method

  1. Cook the rice. Start by cooking the rice according to the packet instructions.
  2. Brown the beef. Meanwhile, heat a skillet to medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it into small pieces as it cooks.
  3. Make the sauce. While the beef is cooking, mix together the soy sauce, honey, sriracha, beef broth, sesame oil, rice vinegar, cornstarch, ginger and garlic in a bowl.
  4. Combine. Add the sliced onions and sauce to the cooked beef. Bring to a simmer and cook until the sauce has thickened and the onions are softened.
  5. Fry the eggs. Heat a separate skillet over high heat. Add the oil, green onions and chilli crisp, then crack an egg over the top. Cook until the white is set but the yolk is still runny. If needed, add a splash of water and cover with a lid to help the white cook through without overdoing the yolk.
  6. Assemble. Serve the rice topped with the beef mixture and a fried egg. Garnish with extra green onion and sesame seeds.
Donna's Tip 💜

Don't skip the eggs cooked with the spring onions and chilli crisp! The oil becomes infused with flavour, the edges of the egg get slightly crispy, and the runny yolk mixed through the beef and rice takes this dish to the next level. Add an extra egg on top if you want to boost the protein even further.
